WHO CAN BE PERSUADED (85 VOTE FLIPS, 24 CASES)

Every flip is argued in the transcript, not announced — and the pattern is philosophy-consistent: the persuadable flip, the anchored don't.

The minimalist (decide narrowly, avoid the big question) flipped 21 times; the textualist ("the text controls, full stop") flipped once. The personas hold under pressure — persuasion is real. It just isn't accurate: eloquent majorities pulled correct independent votes into 5–4 splits on cases the real Court decided 9–0.

THE TAKEAWAY FOR MULTI-AGENT BUILDERS

If your agents can be talked out of correct answers, deliberation is a liability. Independence is a feature you have to engineer for — private votes before public statements made the drift measurable here, and the measurement says the silent jury beat the deliberating panel by ten points. Deliberation bought richer arguments, philosophy-true persuasion, and an audit trail — it did not buy accuracy. Measure both before you pay for debate.
